Smart Mobility Hub

Currently, there are no enhanced mobility or multimodal transit features to alleviate first mile/last mile challenges in the Linden area or along the Cleveland Ave. corridor. Columbus is working to make mobility a great equalizer, in part by embracing multi-modal transportation and making it as accessible and easy to use as possible. The city plans to outfit the hubs with kiosks to assist in travel planning and expanded transportation options via other modes, such as bike and car-sharing. The smart mobility hubs will be linked with COTA systems to provide transit information with real-time arrival and departure times to the passengers waiting at the hubs.

Linden Transit Center

In 1999, COTA built its first Transit Center in Linden. The Transit Center offers child care, banking, and information riders need for taking the bus.

Cleveland Avenue Corridor

The geographic scope of the proposed HUBS project deployment includes the Cleveland Avenue corridor coinciding with COTA's Bus Rapid Transit (CMAX) service and the Easton commercial district, providing those in the Linden area better access to jobs and services in the Easton and Downtown Columbus Commercial districts.
